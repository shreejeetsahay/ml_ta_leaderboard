Traffic Light Autoencoder

In this homework, you will design and train a convolutional autoencoder to compress and reconstruct traffic light images provided from real-world driving scenarios.

Imagine this: You're developing an AI system for autonomous vehicles that need to quickly and efficiently process visual data from traffic lights.

Your task is to build a compact autoencoder model that can compress high-resolution images of traffic lights into a small latent representation and then accurately reconstruct the original images from this compressed form.

Here's the exciting part: you'll wrestle with tradeoffs between model size, overall reconstruction quality, and quality of reconstruction around the traffic light regions, and will be competing on the leaderboard.

Ask yourself: How small can I make my model while still achieving high-quality reconstructions?

The Dataset: Your Playground

We provide a dataset containing 14034 images captured by a roof-mounted stereo camera on a moving vehicle, recorded under various lighting and weather conditions during the day. In this version, only the left-camera view is utilized, so stereo information is not currently used.

Your Mission

  • Build a model that can reconstruct these images. Please make sure that you DO NOT change the IMG_SIZE. Please keep it as 256, and use RGB channel only.
  • Model must be < 23 MB on disk — think small, dream big!

How Will You Be Judged?

  • Metrics tracked:
    • Latent Dimension (LD) — size of the bottleneck vector (lower raw value is better).
    • Full MSE — reconstruction MSE over the entire 256×256 frame (lower is better).
    • ROI-MSE — MSE computed only over Region of Interest pixels (traffic-light area), after resizing boxes to 256×256 and masking to the area. (lower is better)
    • Model Size (MB) — uploaded .pt file size (lower is better; hard cap < 23 MB).
  • Composite (Higher = Better): We convert each “lower-is-better” metric to a normalized score in [0,1] using an inverse scaling score = 1 − (x − min) / (max − min), then combine:
    Weighted Score = 0.40·LD′ + 0.35·FullMSE′ + 0.20·ROI′ + 0.05·Size′
    (LD′, FullMSE′, ROI′, Size′ are the inverted, normalized scores; 1.0 = best, 0.0 = worst.) Normalization bounds- latent_dim: [8, 256]; full_mse: [5e-4, 5e-2]; roi_mse: [2e-3, 8e-2]; model_size (MB): [1.0, 23.0]
  • Sorting & Tie-breakers: Leaderboards sort by Weighted Score (desc). Ties are broken by, in order: 1) lower LD, 2) lower Full MSE, 3) lower ROI-MSE, 4) lower model size, 5) earlier submission time.
  • Submission policy: max 1 submission per team every 15 minutes; file size must be < 23 MB.
